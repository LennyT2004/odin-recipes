Griot

Description

Haitian griot is an iconic dish of Haitian cuisine, renowned for its deliciously savory taste. It consists of pieces of pork marinated in a mixture of herbs, spices and citrus fruits, then fried until crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. The marinade, often made with garlic, lemon juice and local seasonings, gives the griot a burst of signature flavors. Typically served with rice and beans, griot represents a traditional Haitian feast, offering an authentic and flavorful culinary experience.

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds of pork (leg or shoulder)
  • 2 French shallots, quartered
  • 2 shallots cut into 3
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 teaspoon chicken broth powder (or 1 maggi)
  • 1 hot scotch bonnet pepper
  • 1 bouquet garni (a few sprigs of parsley and thyme)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 medium onion cut into large rings
  • 2 sour oranges (or two limes)
  • 1 teaspoon of wine vinegar
  • 3-4 cloves (prick the cloves into the hot pepper)
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil

Instructions

Cleaning the meat

  • Cut the meat into cubes of approximately 1½ to 2 inches, taking care to remove the rind
  • Place the pieces in a large bowl
  • Fill the meat bowl with warm water and rub the meat together to thoroughly clean it. Drain the water and repeat the process 2-3 times When the meat is well rinsed, remove excess water and set aside
  • Cut your citrus fruits in half and squeeze them to remove ¾ of the juice and reserve the liquid in a separate bowl
  • Clean your meat using the citrus halves. (P.-S. You don't have need to clean each piece individually)
  • After cleaning your meat, rinse one last time, taking care to remove excess water

Seasoning

  • To the meat, add the citrus juice, wine vinegar, shallots, chicken broth powder, garlic, salt, pieces of French shallot, pieces of onion and good to mix everything
  • Add the bouquet garni and the hot pepper
  • Leave to rest for approximately at least 1 hour in the refrigerator

Cooking

  • In a pot, add the oil
  • Take each piece of meat individually and place them in the cauldron (make sure to put the chili and bouquet garni on top) cover and cook at medium-low for until the water in the meat is completely evaporated
  • When the water has completely evaporated, remove from heat and let sit

Frying

  • Now that the meat is cooked, all that remains is to brown it in oil
  • Cook until a nice golden color (P.-S. To avoid a blackish color, it is not recommended to use marinade in the meat)
