Katsudon

Description

Katsudon is a delectable Japanese dish renowned for its harmonious blend of flavors and textures. This comforting meal centers around a crispy deep-fried pork cutlet, or "tonkatsu," served atop a bed of steamed rice. The cutlet is complemented by a savory and slightly sweet broth made with ingredients like soy sauce, mirin, and dashi. To complete the ensemble, the dish is crowned with a soft, beaten egg that's gently cooked in the residual heat. The result is a delightful amalgamation of crunchy, succulent pork, velvety egg, and umami-laden broth, creating a satisfying and hearty culinary experience.

Ingredients

  • 4 slices of pork
  • 250g of basmati rice
  • 20g of breadcrumbs
  • 1 tablespoon of mirin
  • 4 eggs
  • 40g of flour
  • 1 onion
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Peanut oil

Instructions

  • Bring salted water to the boil and cook the basmati rice for 20 minutes
  • Salt and pepper the pork slices, dip them in flour, in 2 beaten eggs then in breadcrumbs
  • Fry the slices and cut them into strips widthwise
  • In a non-stick wok, brown the chopped onion, 10ml of water, mirin, soy sauce then add the breaded pork slices
  • Cover with the 2 remaining beaten whole eggs
  • Cover and simmer for a few minutes over low heat
  • In large bowls, put the rice, place on top the crispy breaded pork loins garnished with the beaten egg mixture
